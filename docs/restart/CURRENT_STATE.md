- Latest analytic architecture checkpoint: `docs/architecture/arranger_internal_analytics_algorithm_design_v1.md` now defines full portfolio revaluation as the controlling scenario methodology. Arranger scenarios construct complete scenario market states from approved key-rate/driver perturbations; the engine values every position under the base market state and scenario market state; impact is scenario value minus base value; and attribution by position, manager, account, sleeve, theme, scenario, and confidence happens after valuation. Themes are classification/reporting layers, not valuation substitutes. Coverage/confidence language now describes revaluation limitations, substitute-input use, approved mark policies, and review-required treatment. This is documentation-only design work; it does not add Arranger Studio UI, advisor controls, backend endpoints, scenario-engine runtime, covariance/PCA runtime, Docker/deployment changes, live data, external APIs, real data, dependencies, or product behavior changes.
- Latest scenario-basis design checkpoint: `docs/architecture/key_market_state_scenario_basis_design_v1.md` defines key market-state scenario basis models as small, curated, price-like coordinate systems for constructing complete scenario market states. It supports multiple basis models rather than one universal key-rate set, including broad multi-asset, rates/credit/curve, equity/growth/AI, commodity/energy, FX/global macro, and private liquidity/alternatives examples. Basis construction extracts a base basis vector, applies anchor shocks, completes the vector deterministically or with reduced covariance/PCA, validates it, expands it to full market inputs, and stops before full portfolio revaluation begins.
